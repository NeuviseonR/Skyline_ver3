Denied Boarding & Overbooking

In the event that a flight is overbooked, the airline will first call for volunteers to surrender their seats in exchange for compensation. If there are not enough volunteers:

  • Priority Boarding: The airline reserves the right to determine boarding priority based on fare class, frequent flyer status, and check-in time.
  • Compensation: Passengers denied boarding involuntarily are entitled to travel vouchers or cash compensation, meals, and hotel accommodation if the next available flight is the following day.

Baggage Liability and Claims

The airline’s liability for lost, delayed, or damaged baggage is limited under the Montreal Convention to approximately 1,288 Special Drawing Rights (SDR).

  • Exclusions: The airline is not liable for fragile, perishable, or valuable items such as cash, jewelry, computers, and business documents packed in checked baggage.
  • Reporting Timelines:
    • Damaged Baggage: Must be reported in writing within 7 days.
    • Delayed/Lost Baggage: Must be reported within 21 days.

Conduct of Passengers

The airline maintains the right to refuse carriage or remove a passenger for safety and security reasons.

  • Refusal of Carriage: This applies to passengers under the influence of alcohol/drugs or those exhibiting behavior that threatens the crew.
  • Diversion Costs: If a flight is diverted due to passenger misconduct, the airline reserves the right to recover all associated costs from that passenger.

Schedules, Delays, and Substitution

Times shown in timetables are not guaranteed and do not form part of the contract of carriage.

  • Substitution: The airline may substitute alternate carriers or aircraft without prior notice.
  • Force Majeure: The airline is not liable for delays caused by "Acts of God," weather, or air traffic control restrictions.

Successive Carriers

Carriage performed by several successive carriers under one ticket is regarded as a single operation.

  • Claims: For baggage claims, passengers may take action against the first carrier, the last carrier, or the specific carrier where the damage occurred.
